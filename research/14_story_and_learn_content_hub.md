## 📖 2. The 15 Curated Learn Tab Wealth Notes (Fully Written)

These snackable, beautifully written articles explain core personal finance principles under the hood of the app:

### Note 1: The Magic of the Rule of 72
* **Explanation**: The simplest mental math formula to calculate how many years it will take to double your money at a given interest rate.
* **Formula**:
  $$\text{Years to Double} = \frac{72}{\text{Annual Return Rate (CAGR)}}$$
* **Actionable Tip**: If your index mutual fund grows at 12% CAGR, your money will double in exactly 6 years ($72 \div 12 = 6$). If left in a savings account at 3%, it will take 24 years!

### Note 2: The Safe Withdrawal Rate (The 4% Rule)
* **Explanation**: Developed from the Trinity Study, the Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR) is the percentage of your portfolio you can withdraw annually in retirement without running out of money for at least 30 years.
* **Formula**:
  $$\text{Corpus Needed} = \text{Annual Expenses} \times 25 \quad (1 \div 0.04 = 25)$$
* **Actionable Tip**: If your annual expenses are ₹6 Lakhs, your retirement corpus is ₹1.5 Crore. Adjust the withdrawal amount for inflation every year.

### Note 3: What is BaristaFIRE? (Mental Freedom Early)
* **Explanation**: Full FIRE requires covering 100% of your expenses with your portfolio. BaristaFIRE assumes you quit your high-stress job, but work a low-stress, part-time job or consulting gig that covers a portion of your expenses.
* **Formula**:
  $$\text{BaristaFIRE Corpus} = (\text{Annual Expenses} - \text{Side Income}) \times 25$$
* **Actionable Tip**: Earning just ₹20,000/month (₹2.4L/year) reduces your standard ₹3 Crore retirement corpus requirement to ₹2.4 Crore, allowing you to quit stressful corporate jobs 5 to 10 years earlier!

### Note 4: Sequence-of-Returns Risk (The Hidden Danger)
* **Explanation**: The risk that market crashes occur early in your retirement phase. If you retire right before a bear market (like 2008), withdrawing money while your portfolio shrinks can deplete your corpus permanently.
* **Mitigation**: Maintain a 2-year cash/debt buffer (Fixed Deposits) so you don't have to sell depreciated equities during a crash.

### Note 5: Real Inflation vs. Retail Inflation
* **Explanation**: Retail inflation (CPI) sits at ~5.5% in India. However, personal lifestyle inflation (education, health, rent) typically grows at 8-10% annually.
* **Actionable Tip**: Always use at least **6% to 7% inflation** in long-term projections to avoid falling short of purchasing power in your 50s.

### Note 6: The Pitfall of Regular vs. Direct Mutual Funds
* **Explanation**: Regular mutual funds pay recurring trail commissions (1%-1.5% every year) to brokers. Direct mutual funds have zero commissions.
* **Impact**: Over a 20-year horizon, this small 1.5% difference can cost you **up to 25% of your final portfolio value** in commissions! Always buy Direct plans.

### Note 7: LIC/Endowment Traps (Never Mix Insurance & Investment)
* **Explanation**: Traditional LIC/Endowment plans offer life cover + investment returns. However, their net returns rarely beat **5%-6% CAGR**, which lags inflation.
* **Actionable Tip**: Buy a pure Term Life Insurance (cheap, huge cover) and invest the difference directly in Index Mutual Funds.

### Note 8: Why Indian Real Estate Rental Yield is a Trap
* **Explanation**: Rental yields in major Indian Tier-1 cities (Mumbai, Bangalore, Gurgaon) stand at a tiny **2%-3%**, compared to 6%-7% in the US.
* **Impact**: Buying a home purely for rental income, while paying an 8.5% home loan EMI, is a major drag on wealth. Renting is often far more optimal in major Indian metros.

### Note 9: The Danger of Credit Card Rollovers
* **Explanation**: Paying the "Minimum Due" on credit cards rolls over the remaining balance at an annual interest rate of **36% to 42%**.
* **Impact**: This is compound interest working *against* you. Pay off high-interest debt instantly before buying stocks.

### Note 10: Asset Allocation (The Diversification Shield)
* **Explanation**: Spreading capital across Equity, Debt, and Gold. Equity drives long-term growth, Debt provides stability, and Gold hedges against inflation/crises.
* **Rule of Thumb**: $\text{Equity Allocation} = 100 - \text{Your Age}$. Rebalance annually.

### Notes 11 to 15: Fast Financial Wisdom
* **Note 11 (Emergency Fund Sizing)**: Always keep 6 months of mandatory living expenses in highly liquid accounts (sweep-in FDs) before starting a single SIP.
* **Note 12 (Tax-Loss Harvesting in India)**: Under Indian law, Equity LTCG is tax-free up to ₹1 Lakh per financial year. Harvest this annually to reset your cost basis.
* **Note 13 (Nifty 50 Index Investing)**: Low expense ratios, zero human fund manager risk. The simplest way to beat 85% of active mutual funds.
* **Note 14 (The Magic of Compounding)**: Compounding is slow at first, but explosive after year 15. The real growth happens in the tail end of the projection.
* **Note 15 (Indian New Tax Regime Slab Mastery)**: Simplified tax brackets without deduction headaches. Keep calculations simple by default.
